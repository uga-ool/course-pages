Course Description

Examination of the ways organizations satisfy consumer and organizational needs and wants for products and services. Emphasis is placed on how the elements of the marketing mix are used to provide customer satisfaction in the marketing of goods and services, both domestically and internationally.

Prerequisites

Successful completion of ACCT 2101 and (MIST 2090 or CSCI 1100-1100L). Note: Students enrolled in 3001 are exempt from prerequisites.

Final class rosters will be reviewed after Drop/Add. Any student not meeting prerequisites will be administratively withdrawn from the class.

Course Objectives | Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, the student should be able to:

  • Define and explain the marketing concept and related terminology.
  • Explain the importance of target markets and understand segmentation and positioning.
  • Understand and be able to apply the elements of the marketing mix.
  • Explain the importance of and the basic methodologies of marketing research.
  • Know the differences between domestic and international marketing environments and understand how strategies need to be adjusted.
  • Identify and appropriately respond to ethical issues faced by marketing managers.

Topical Outline

  • Definition of Marketing and the Marketing Concept
  • Marketing Environment including current demographic and economic data
  • Marketing ethics including AMA code of ethics
  • Introduction to marketing research
  • Buyer Behavior
  • Target Marketing and Segmentation
  • Product issues
  • Pricing issues
  • Promotional issues
  • Place issues
  • International Marketing
  • Services Marketing
  • Marketing planning
  • On-line Marketing Issues

Management by the Numbers Exercises

You must complete the two modules listed below. The time required to complete each module will vary depending on your background experience and comfort level with quantitative analyses. Therefore, you are strongly encouraged to begin working on the first tutorial as early as possible. You will need to familiarize yourself with the content by viewing and possibly taking notes on the tutorial, prior to beginning each problem set. Before getting started, you will need to go through the “Intro” tutorial (a flash presentation). It will help you get acquainted with the format of the questions and how to submit your answers before you begin the actual problem sets.

There are four problem sets for each module. Each problem set consists of 4-8 questions based on a realistic business situation. You will have two opportunities to answer each question correctly before advancing. After you have attempted to answer all the questions for a particular problem set, you will move to the next problem set with a new business situation. Generally, you will find the first questions in a problem set less complex so that you can build toward more difficult questions at the end of the problem set.

Completion of a problem set means that you have answered all the questions correctly in a row ('mastery'). Once you have completed four problem sets, you have completed that module. The average of your scores on the two modules will be your assignment score for Module 6.
